Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find what you're looking for? Contact us directly for more information.

Our main farm and offices are located off Thika Road in Nairobi. We have distribution points in several major towns across Kenya including Kisumu, Eldoret, and Mombasa.

Our offices are open Monday to Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and Saturdays from 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM. We're closed on Sundays and public holidays.

We accept cash on delivery, M-Pesa payments, and bank transfers. For large orders, we may require a deposit before processing your order.

Our egg prices vary depending on market conditions and order quantity. As of the current date, a tray of eggs costs KSh 300-350. Please contact our sales team for the most up-to-date pricing.

Yes, all our day-old chicks come vaccinated against common poultry diseases including Marek's Disease, Newcastle Disease, and Infectious Bronchitis. We provide a vaccination schedule with each purchase.

We stock a range of high-quality feeds for different growth stages:

  • Starter feed (0-3 weeks)
  • Grower feed (4-6 weeks)
  • Finisher feed (7 weeks to market)
  • Layer feed (for egg production)
  • Broiler feed (for meat production)

Yes, we deliver to all major towns and cities in Kenya. Delivery charges vary depending on location and order size. We offer free delivery within Nairobi for orders above KSh 5,000.

Delivery times vary by location:

  • Nairobi: Same day for orders placed before 12pm
  • Other major towns: 1-2 business days
  • Upcountry areas: 2-3 business days (depending on our delivery schedule)

Yes, customers are welcome to pick up orders from our Nairobi facility. Please arrange pickup in advance so we can have your order ready. Pickup hours are Monday-Saturday, 8am-4pm.

For beginners, we recommend:

  • Broilers: Fast-growing with good feed conversion
  • Kuroiler: Hardy dual-purpose breed good for meat and eggs
  • Kenbro: Another hardy dual-purpose breed
Our team can help you choose based on your specific goals and location.

Space requirements vary by age and type:

  • Day-old chicks: 50 chicks per square meter
  • Growing birds: 10-12 birds per square meter
  • Layers: 5-7 birds per square meter
Proper ventilation is equally important as space.

Yes, we conduct regular training sessions for both beginners and experienced farmers. Topics include:

  • Brooding management
  • Feeding programs
  • Disease prevention
  • Business aspects of poultry farming
Check our blog for upcoming training dates or contact us for one-on-one consultations.
