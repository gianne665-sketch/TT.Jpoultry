Essential Tips for Raising Healthy Chicks

Expert advice for successful brooding and chick care

June 15, 2023 5 min read Chicken Care

Raising healthy chicks from day-old to productive chickens requires proper care, especially during the critical first few weeks. At TT&J Poultry, we've helped thousands of Kenyan farmers successfully raise their flocks. Here are our expert tips for ensuring your chicks grow into healthy, productive chickens.

1. Proper Brooding Setup

The brooding period (first 3-4 weeks) is crucial for chick survival and development. Set up your brooder at least 24 hours before the chicks arrive.

Brooder Requirements:

  • Space: 50 chicks per square meter in first week, gradually increasing space as they grow
  • Heat source: Infrared bulbs or brooder heaters (1 per 100 chicks)
  • Bedding: 5-10cm of clean, dry wood shavings (avoid sawdust for young chicks)
  • Ventilation: Fresh air without drafts
  • Light: 24 hours of light for first week, then reduce gradually

A well-set brooder with proper heat lamps and adequate space

2. Temperature Management

Maintaining correct brooder temperature is essential for chick health and growth.

3. Feeding and Watering

Proper nutrition from day one sets the foundation for healthy growth.

Feeding Guidelines:

  • Use high-quality starter feed (20-22% protein) for first 3 weeks
  • Feed should be finely ground or crumbled for easy eating
  • Provide feed in shallow trays for first few days, then transition to feeders
  • Feed should be available at all times (ad libitum)
  • Add vitamins and electrolytes to water for first 3-5 days to reduce stress

Pro Tip:

For the first 24 hours, sprinkle feed on paper or cardboard to help chicks learn to eat. Gently tap the surface to attract their attention.

4. Health Management

Preventing disease is easier than treating outbreaks. Follow these health practices:

1

Vaccination

Follow recommended vaccination schedule for Marek's, Newcastle, Gumboro, and other diseases common in your area.

2

Biosecurity

Limit visitors to your brooder area, disinfect footwear, and keep wild birds away.

3

Clean Water

Provide clean, fresh water daily. Clean and disinfect waterers every 2-3 days.

4

Observation

Check chicks daily for signs of illness (lethargy, drooping wings, closed eyes, abnormal droppings).

5. Gradual Transition to Grower Feed

At 3-4 weeks, gradually transition chicks to grower feed (18-20% protein):

100% grower feed

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcrowding

Leads to stress, disease, and uneven growth. Follow recommended space requirements.

Inconsistent Temperature

Causes chilling or heat stress. Use thermometers and monitor behavior.

Poor Sanitation

Dirty brooders spread disease. Clean and disinfect between batches.

Late Vaccination

Follow schedule precisely for effective immunity.

Final Thoughts

Raising healthy chicks requires attention to detail in the first critical weeks. By providing proper heat, nutrition, and care, you'll establish a strong foundation for your flock's future productivity. Remember that happy, comfortable chicks grow into productive chickens.
